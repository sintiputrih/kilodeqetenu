Chumlee | Real Estate, Cars, and Shoe Collection

In 2012, Chumlee lashed our $1.1 million to purchase a lavish mansion in Las Vegas.

Surprisingly, the sales price is nearly half a million shy of its original listed price of $1.85 million.

Car Collection

As someone who gets to valuate and admire all different types of automobiles on a regular basis, Chumlee has built a tasty car collection.

Moreover, the show regularly features his vintage 1986 Buick Regal, which comes equipped with hydraulic lifts and only three wheels.

The price for a 1986 Buick Regal in a decent condition today without any hydraulic mods and all four wheels starts from $10,000.

The other classic American muscle in Chumlee’s garage is the 1964 Chevy Impala.

Like the Buick, it’s another lowrider and is in top-notch condition considering its age.

A more modern eye-catcher and on the higher end of the pawn star’s garage sits the Maserati Gran Turismo.

The Italian Supercar comes with a staggering price tag of $150,000.

But we’re pretty sure that Chum used his world-famous negotiating skills to save a couple of grand while purchasing this supreme Maserati product.

Coming to the more practical side of the spectrum, Mr. Lee also owns a couple of SUVs, the Range Rover Evoque and the Cadillac Escalade, for which he paid $65,000 and $75,000, respectively.

Sneaker Collection

Chumlee | TV Shows, Investments, Endorsements

TV Shows

Pawn Stars

Chumlee has been an integral part of the show since its first season, and that’s not just because of his immense popularity among the fans.

As a top-notch negotiator, he’s made tons of profitable deals for the shop, while there are a couple of setbacks to be noted.

In season 8, Chum negotiated a 1960 Spacelander bike from $20,000 down to $8,000.

Rick Harrison was satisfied with the deal so much that he gave Chumlee his first $1,000 bonus.

The bike was later sold for $20,000.

Similarly, in the 14th season of the show, he purchased a set of three samurai swords for around $4,000.

Later on, the swords were valued to be around $13,000.

Not a bad day at the office for Chum, eh!

However, his most profitable deal was yet to come.

In season 15, he spent more than $10,000 on a Beatles record collection which was immediately sold out for nearly double the price.
