Jeremy Doku reveals plans to return to Ghana

Manchester City winger Jeremy Doku will visit Ghana, the country of his parents, in the near future.

Doku has not been to Ghana since his early childhood when his parents brought him to the country of their births.

In 2020, Doku chose to represent Belgium after talks with then-Ghana coach Charles Akonnor.

Speaking to Premier League TV, the 21-year-old who speaks fluent Twi, said he would return to Ghana, sooner rather than later.

“Yeah. Of course I’m Ghanaian. My parents are also Ghanaians. I also speak Twi which is the language of Ghana. So I have to go back again because its (been) a long time ago.”

In an disclosure that did not surprise many, Doku also revealed his admiration for Ghanaian music and dance.

“I like Ghana. I like the way they talk. I the the music.”

Doku who has scored on two occassions in eighteen appearances for Belgium, says a return to the country of his parents is on the horizon.

“My parents often go back. I haven’t gone back yet so I have to go.”
