Aryna Sabalenka Engaged To Her Husband To Be Konstantin Koltsov, Kids Family And Net Worth

Belarusian tennis player Aryna Sabalenka is engaged to her boyfriend, Konstantin Koltsov. This article will cover more about her Kids, Family And Net Worth; continue reading.

Belarusian professional tennis player Aryna Sabalenka is the recipient of two Grand Slam doubles titles, the 2019 U.S. Open and the 2021 Australian Open.

The tennis pro rose to fame by leading the Belarus Fed Cup team to a runner-up finish with Aliaksandra Sasnovich in 2017.

As of 2023, her Wikipedia claims she has won 17 career titles, eleven in singles and six in doubles.

Famed for her aggressive style of play, Aryna has been ranked as high as world No. 1 in doubles and world No. 2 in singles by the Women’s Tennis Association (WTA).

Is Aryna Sabalenka Engaged To Konstantin Koltsov?

Yes,Aryna Sabalenka is dating Konstantin Koltsov.

Although several online media claims they are engaged, factual information is unavailable at the moment, and neither has Aryana disclosed anything on her social media.

Meanwhile, her fans can observe several cozy pictures of Aryana with Konstantin Koltsov, which she has shared among her 393 k followers on her Instagram [email protected]_aryna.

Her partner, Konstantin, is a former Belarusian professional ice hockey player who last played for HC Dinamo Minsk in the Kontinental Hockey League (KHL).

Konstantin played for the Belarusian national team at the 2002 and 2010 Winter Olympics and nine World Championships.

According to Sportskeeda, Aryna and Konstantin have been together since the initial months of 2021.

Before dating Sabalenka, Koltsov was happily married to his wife, Yulia; they called off their wedding after things went sour between them in 2020. 

Do Aryna Sabalenka Have Kids?

Aryna’s love for kids is evident in her Instagram posts that she has shared among followers.

Although she is not a mother yet, her Instagram post with a kid, which she uploaded on April 8, 2023, raises several speculations that she has a kid.

In 2015, the Belarusian Tennis Federation persuaded Aryana and her team to focus on playing low-level professional events instead of junior tournaments, even though she was still eligible to compete at the junior level.
