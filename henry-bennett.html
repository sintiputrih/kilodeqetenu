Biography

Henry Bennett was born in Arkansas on December 14, 1886. After serving as Superintendent of Schools in two Oklahoma counties, Bennett became President of the Oklahoma Agricultural and Mechanical College. He later rose to national prominence, serving as the United States Assistant Secretary of State. He was appointed by President Harry S. Truman to a federal foreign development project called the Point Four Program. While attending Ouachita Baptist College in Arkadelphia, Arkansas, he managed the school’s yearbook; headed a literary club; and was elected to a high-ranking management position within the college’s athletic organization.
