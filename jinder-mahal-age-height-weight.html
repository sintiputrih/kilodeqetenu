Jinder Mahal Age, Height, Weight

Jinder Mahal Age, Height, Weight: Jinder Mahal is a Canadian professional wrestler, officially known as Yuvraj Singh Dhesi.

He developed a passion for wrestling at a younger age, has been consistent throughout his career, and has become one of the most sought-after wrestlers.

Jinder Mahal Age

Jinder Mahal celebrated his 37th birthday in June this year (2023). He was born on Saturday, July 19, 1986, in Calgary, Canada

Jinder Mahal Height & Weight

Jinder Mahal stands at a height of 1.96 m and weighs around 108 kg. He holds a business degree in communications and culture from the University of Calgary

As of this writing, (Sunday, December 3, 2023), he’s signed to WWE, where he performs on the Raw brand as the leader of the Indus Sher stable.

Born in Calgary, Canada, Mahal began his professional wrestling career at the Martial Arts Fitness Center in Calgary.

He debuted in Premier Martial Arts Wrestling (PMW) as ‘Raj Dhesi’. He won various championships in Stampede and Prairie Wrestling Alliance (PWA)

In the Prairie Wrestling Alliance (PWA), Mahal was the PWA Heavyweight Champion from 2008 until January 2010.

Having started his career on the independent circuit, Mahal joined WWE in 2010, debuting on the company’s main roster the following year.

Mahal made his televised WWE debut on the April 29, 2011, episode of SmackDown. He was released from the WWE in 2014 and returned in 2016.

Mahal won the WWE United States Championship at WrestleMania 34 in 2018 and the WWE 24/7 Championship twice in 2019.

On January 27, 2019, Mahal participated in the Royal Rumble match, entering at number 7, before being eliminated by Johnny Gargano

He competed in the Andre the Giant Memorial Battle Royal, but failed to win. He was moved to the SmackDown brand as part of Superstar Shake-up.

Mahal appeared at the Superstar Spectacle on January 26, 2021, reuniting with The Singh Brothers but lost in a six-man tag team match

In October 2021, he entered the King of the Ring tournament, where he defeated Kofi Kingston in the first round, but lost to Xavier Woods in the semi-finals

In November 2021, Dhesi announced he was joining the ABC drama series Big Sky cast, debuting on the 22nd episode (“Heart-shaped Charm”) as Dhruv.

On the April 15, 2022, edition of SmackDown, Mahal challenged American wrestler, Ricochet for the Intercontinental Championship but was unsuccessful.

In January 2023, Mahal returned to NXT for the first time since 2012, aligning himself with Indus Sher (Veer and Sanga) by attacking The Creed Brothers

On the February 21 episode of NXT, Mahal challenged Bron Breakker for the NXT Championship in a losing effort.

As part of the 2023 WWE Draft, Mahal returned to the main roster as Indus Sher collectively was drafted to the Raw brand

Jinder Mahal has appeared in the wrestling video games WWE ’13, WWE 2K14, WWE 2K18, WWE 2K19, WWE 2K20, WWE 2K22 and WWE 2K23.
