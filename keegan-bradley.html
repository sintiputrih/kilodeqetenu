Kenneth Hansen Bradley, also known as Keegan Bradley, is a professional golfer from the United States who plays on the PGA Tour. He participated on the American national team in the 2012 Ryder Cup and won the 2011 PGA Championship and Rookie of the Year Award.

In 2008, he became a professional, and he started his career on the NGA Hooters Tour. At the Byron Nelson Championship in May 2013, Keegan shot an incredible 60, falling just one shot short of joining the select group of players who shot 59. At the Travelers Championship in June 2019, Bradley tied for second place with Zack Sucher after shooting a week-low -13.

What is the Professional Career of Keegan Bradley?

In his fifth and final start of the year on the NGA Hooters Tour, Keegan Bradley won his first tournament in the professional game there. In 26 competitions that season, he missed 22 cuts, earning $84,000. After failing by two strokes in his effort to qualify for the 2010 PGA Tour, he played on the Nationwide Tour in 2010. There, he produced four straight top-five finishes late in the season to finish 14th on the money list and secure his 2011 PGA Tour card. He also qualified for his first PGA Tour event, the 2011 Sony Open in Hawaii, and placed T-7 in the Bob Hope Classic the following week. At the HP Byron Nelson Championship in 2011, he triumphed over Ryan Palmer in a sudden-death playoff to claim his first PGA Tour victory.

Bradley competed in his maiden major at the 2011 PGA Championship and won the three-hole playoff with a birdie and two pars, finishing one stroke ahead of Dufner, who had a round of par, bogey, and birdie. He also became the first golfer to win a major with a long putter and only the third player after Francis Ouimet (1913) and Ben Curtis (2003) to win a major on his first attempt. Keegan also became the first American major champion since Phil Mickelson won the 2010 Masters and the seventh consecutive first-time major winner.
In December, he received the ninth PGA Tour Rookie of the Year award.

More on Career

While playing in Europe for the first time at The Irish Open at Royal Portrush in 2012, he lost a two-stroke lead in the final round of the WGC-Cadillac Championship, losing four strokes over the final four holes. He missed the cut in June of the following year. Keegan finished one shot ahead of Jim Furyk and Steve Stricker to take home his third PGA Tour victory at the 2012 WGC-Bridgestone Invitational. The following week, when he tied for third at the 2012 PGA Championship, he improved to a career-high of 12th. He therefore earned a spot in the 2012 Ryder Cup, making history as the first player to compete in a cup match.

He placed second twice in 2013; the first time was at the HP Byron Nelson Championship in May, where he scored a round-record 60 at the TPC Four Seasons. At the WGC-Bridgestone Invitational at Firestone in August 2013, he came in second place once more, this time trailing Tiger Woods by seven shots. He also competed for the United States on the Presidents Cup winning squad in Ohio’s Muirfield Village. Also, He had his weakest season since joining the PGA Tour in 2015, finishing in second place at the Arnold Palmer Invitational at Bay Hill.

He failed to represent the United States in a year-end international tournament for the first time in four years. Keegan played poorly; he failed to claim a title and placed 114th and 51st on the PGA Tour money list. In 2018, he won the FedEx Cup Playoffs event known as the BMW Championship. He placed eighth in the FedEx Cup for the entire season and earned a spot in the season-ending Tour Championship. In June 2019, he tied for second place with Zack Sucher at the Travelers Championship after shooting a week-low -13.
