Biography

Michael Bishop was born in Nebraska on November 12, 1945. An American writer who published over thirty works of science fiction and fantasy, he is best known for novels such as A Funeral for the Eyes of Fire; Stolen Faces; Transfigurations; and Under Heaven’s Bridge. His later works include No Enemy but Time, Ancient of Days, and Unicorn Mountain. He wrote an introduction to a book by Mary Shelley. After graduating from the University of Georgia, he worked as a freelance writer and a substitute teacher.
