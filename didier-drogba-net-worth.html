Career

Drogba’s Club Career

The Ivorian striker Didier Drogba began his professional career in January 2001 with French team Le Mans FC.

The following year he signed for the Ligue 1 team Guingamp for $85,000 and scored 17 goals in 34 games.

Evidently, This drew the interest of Olympique de Marseille, who signed him for a price of $4 million.

Furthermore, Drogba only stayed in Marseille for a year, scoring 19 goals before joining Chelsea.

Besides, Marseilles gained a lot of money off Drogba’s transfer since they charged $27 million.

However, in the years afterward, Drogba has proven that he was worth every penny Chelsea spent for him.

Chelsea won the league title for the first time in 50 years during Drogba’s first season at the club despite a sluggish start.

Nonetheless, Drogba truly came to his true potential during the 2006-07 season, when he scored 33 goals in 38 league games.

Similarly, In the same year, he also won the Golden Boot of the English Premier League.

After Mourinho departed Chelsea in 2007, Drogba was left uncertain about the future at Chelsea; he stayed devoted to the club, regardless.

Moreover, Drogba’s excellent level of performance in crucial matches is a major part of his reputation.

Additionally, in May 2012, he scored the game-winning penalty kick against Bayern Munich to help Chelsea win their first-ever UEFA Champions League.

The next season, Drogba left for Shanghai Shenhua, then Galatasaray, then Montreal Impact, and Arizona Phoenix Rising finally.

Surprisingly, he made a short homecoming to Chelsea for a season (2014/15).

International Career

Didier Drogba led his country to its first World Cup appearance in 2006.

Further, He also guided the club to the African Cup of Nations final that year.

Moreover, The Ivorian striker earned 105 caps for his country and scored 65 goals while wearing an Ivory Coast jersey.

After the 2014 FIFA World Cup, The goal machine Drogba declared his retirement from international football.
