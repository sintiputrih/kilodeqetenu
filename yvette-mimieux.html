    Yvette Mimieux Career

    Jim Byron who was drawn by her beauty spotted her and suggested she become an actress. Her first acting appearances were in episodes of the TV show Yancy Derringer and One Step Beyond. Mimieux’s successful career started in 1895 when she attained her first role in The Time Machine film where she played the character Weena.

    Rod Taylor starred in the movie. In the same year, she received much attention and admiration when she depicted a teenage character on vacation in the movie,’ Where The Boys Are’.

    However, for the following years of her acting career, she appeared in movies that were critically and commercially slacken. But that did not put her down. She regained her fame in 1976 when she appeared in the movie ‘Jackson County Jail’.

    Mimieux then played the role of Dinah in the movie ‘Outside Chance’.In 1979, she landed the role of Dr.Kate McCrae in the movie ‘The Black Hole. Not forgetting the central role she had in ‘Light In The Piazza when Mimieux played a mentally disabled girl.
