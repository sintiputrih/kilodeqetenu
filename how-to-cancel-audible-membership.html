How to Cancel Audible Membership in Easy Steps

If you like the idea of a good read is finding the nearest comfortable place and putting on headphones, you are most likely known with Audible, Amazon’s subscription audiobook and podcast service. 

For signing up, Audible allows a free 30-day trial, where you can easily explore the world of fictional storytelling. For those interested, it’s also worth stating that Audible provides free books for your listening pleasure.

But let’s be honest, Sometimes our ambitious audiobook plans do not work over so well. And in the end, you might want to remove that Audible subscription before the free trial is even over. 

Whether you are a newcomer or a long-time audience who is not getting the most out of the subscription, canceling an Audible membership is accessible from Audible’s desktop site.

Audible is a subscription service owned by Amazon that produces and sells audiobooks, audio versions of newspapers and magazines, and TV and radio programs. 

It is important to note: If you cancel your membership, you automatically lose any unspent credits still in your account, so It would be better to either pause your membership or spend these credits before you cancel. Suppose you still decide to cancel after paying your remaining credits. In that case, you will always have access to your audiobooks through your account on any device, so you can still access your Library and listen at any time.

Simply uninstalling the Audible app from your phone or tablet will not delete your membership. Likewise, canceling your membership is much simpler from a computer, but you can perform the steps below from your phone or tablet, as long as you are using the device’s web browser.

Here are a few steps to cancel an Audible subscription.

How to Cancel Audible Membership

1. Visit its website audible.com and sign in using your Amazon account information.

2. Once you are signed in, go to your personalized greeting and press “Account details” from the dropdown menu.

3. On your account page, go to click on “Cancel membership.” This will start the cancellation process.

4. The cancellation process comprises a few pages that help you to reconsider canceling your membership. Just follow the instructions and hit on “Continue canceling.” 

5. On the final screen, verify your cancellation by pressing the “Finish Canceling” button.

6. You will know you have succeeded if you see a “We’re sorry to see you go” message that has arrived at the top of your account’s details page. You will also get an email telling you that your Audible subscription has been canceled.

How to Hold Audible Membership

If you just want to take a brief break, Audible Plus and Audible Premium Plus members have the choice to put their account on hold.

Audible memberships can be put on hold once every 12 months and up to 3 months of period. Just note that only Audible Premium Plus members can access their Library during a hold. 

To put your account on hold, you will need to contact Audible directly:

1. Go to Audible’s Contact Us page

2. press “Membership,” and underneath “Tell us more,” choose “Switch, Pause, or Cancel Membership” from the dropdown menu. 

3. Then, pick your chosen communication method — chat, phone, or email — to chat with a customer service representative to review your account and go over plan options.

You will not get a refund after you cancel your subscription. Your member benefits will also end the moment you close your account. Still, you will be able to access your profile and listen to the books you bought before the cancelation.
