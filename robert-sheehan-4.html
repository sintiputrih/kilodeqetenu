Career

Robert Sheehan started his career as an actor during his primary school when he participated in the play titled Oliver with the Twist in which he played the role of Oliver. After that, he started giving auditions and bagged his first role at the age of fourteen when he made his appearance in the movie titled Song for a Raggy Boy in the year 2003.

Later he joined the Laois Open Door theatre group and worked in the play titled The Cripple of Insihmaan playing the role of a crippled boy. In the year 2004, he made his appearance in the popular Australian television show titled Foreign Exchange when he was just 16 years old.

After that, he has worked in several other television series like The Clinic, The Tudors, Young Blades, Rock Rivals, and Bitter Sweet. He also worked in a movie titled Ghostwood during this period.

After completing his graduation, he made his appearance in the popular British drama film titled Cherrybomb in the year 2009. In the same year, he has worked in the television series titled Red Riding. After that, he bagged the lead role of Nathan Young in the popular British television series titled Misfits.

In the year 2010, he made his appearance in the popular Irish television series titled Love/Hate. Since then, he has worked in several popular television series like The Borrowers, Accused, Me and Mrs. Jones, Fortitude, The Young Offenders, The Umbrella Academy, and several others.

He has also worked in several popular movies like Season of the Witch, Demons Never Die, The Mortal Instruments: City of Bones, The Road Within, Three Summers, Bad Samaritan, Mortal Engines, and others.
